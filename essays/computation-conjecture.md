# Computation Conjecture

This conjecture, or bold guess, is based on a simple idea: to making progress on fundamental ontology, or exploring the true nature of existence, we can use lessons found by studying computers. That's the idea. I'm claiming computers hold insights that can illuminate the cosmos. There are many beliefs about the fundamental nature of the universe, for some matter is all there is, for others mind is fundamental, and still for others God is answer to the final question. I seek to harmonize these positions. We'll start by exploring the transitive properties of computation and how they can interact with physics, argue why physicalism is false, show a harmony between mind and matter, showcase a few thought experiments for depth, and make conjectures about the true nature of reality.

## Computers

Since we've had computers for a while its easy to take their lessons for granted. Many, including me, wait for the next technological breakthrough: AGI, quantum computing, matieral sciences, robots, or fission. Yet, have we understood the meaning of what we already have? I don't think so. For example, my children love to play Minecraft. In September 2010, "theinternetftw" built a 16-bit ALU (Arithmetic Logic Unit) inside of Minecraft. That was the first computer built within Minecraft. In 2021, the most famous Minecraft example, Sammyuri developed "CHUNGUS 2", an 8-bit computer that can play games like Snake, and later in 2022 the same group built a 2D Minecraft within Minecraft. The point here is simple: these are computers within computers. For students of computers, this is the Universal Turing Machine proposed by Alan Turning in his 1936 paper, "On Computable Numbers, with an Application to the Entscheidungsproblem."

What makes it possible create a computer within a computer also makes it possible for arbitrary depth of computers within computers. What is this property? It is the processing of basic logicical operations: AND, OR, and NOT. In modern computers, these are typically simplified into NAND gates since you can get the features of of AND, OR, and NOT in one logical gate. When you build a computer program, e.g. Minecraft and provide it with the ability to process logic gates, e.g. Redstone, then you make it possible to build a computer within a computer. There is no principled limit to this nesting.

Minecraft is a program that runs on a computer. This is important because it means that we have two ideas that we'll use throughout this essay: computer and program. Notice that in Minecraft after Redstone (logic gates) was added, it allowed for the creation of a computer within the game. This means a program can itself be a computer within a computer. In fact, computers do not need to be made of silico at all. They can be made of any substrates that supports processing logic gates such as water/pipes, dominatos, and mechanical devices.

Just as Alan Turing argued, there exists a UTM (Universal Turing Machine).

> Claim #1 - Computers support arbitrarily nested computers. Computers are logic processing programs.
