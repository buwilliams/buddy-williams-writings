## Important Conjectures

**On fallibilism and realism:**
- Fallibilism entails realism — to be wrong about something, that something must actually exist
- The laws of physics (and moral facts) exist in an objective, ontological sense, independent of our knowledge of them
- Newton's theory is strictly false — working well in a domain does not make it true

**On the trilemma:**
- There are exactly three positions: fallibilism, dogmatism, and relativism
- Dogmatism and relativism are logically equivalent in the relevant respect — both deny the possibility of being objectively wrong
- Fallibilism is the only non-dogmatic, non-relativist option
- Relativism and fallibilism strictly contradict each other — you cannot blend them

**On moral objectivity:**
- The multiverse contains all physically possible choices; some are objectively better than others
- Moral theories (utilitarianism, deontology, etc.) are not foundations but critiques — heuristics for error correction
- Better moral choices tend to correct errors faster, create more knowledge and wealth, and preserve the means of future error correction

**Deutsch's unification:**
- "Do not destroy the means of error correction" simultaneously makes a moral claim and an epistemological claim, unifying the two domains
- All evils are caused by insufficient knowledge (Deutsch's principle of optimism)

**On moral relativism's practical harm:**
- Relativism blocks problem-solving by denying that some things are problems
- It infects science as well as ethics (e.g., denying biological sex as an objective category)
- Enlightenment liberal societies are objectively morally better than alternatives like Islamic theocracy
