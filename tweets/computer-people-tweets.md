# Computer People - Tweet Thread

---

1/14

Computer people (AGI) are nearly here. This is a synthesis of two years of research: what they are, what they can do, and what follows. Eight claims. Each rests on an argument. The thread is the argument chain.

---

2/14

Explanation space is large. An explanation of relativity fits in under 1,000 words. With 50,000 words available, the combinatorial space for explanations is 10^5,000, exceeding the 10^80 atoms in the observable universe. Every human breakthrough lives in this space. So do breakthroughs not yet made.

---

3/14

LLMs have access to ~100,000 words and context windows of ~1,000,000 words. Their combinatorial space is 10^5,000,000. The question is not whether they have access to this space. The question is whether they can search it non-randomly, guided by problems and evaluated by judgment.

---

4/14

All breakthroughs happen in abstract space. Fallibilism: knowledge grows through conjecture and criticism, not observation. No mind accesses reality directly. We work with theory-laden conjectures. LLMs are immersed in the same abstract structures that ground human judgment.

---

5/14

Progress is problem-directed reconnection. Problems emerge through surprise, when explanations fail. We reconnect known structures in novel ways to resolve the surprise. Reasoning models solve novel problems not in their training data. That is not retrieval. That is the work of judgment.

---

6/14

AGI is a universal explainer: a system with the minimum capabilities to learn and make genuine scientific progress. Frontier models meet this bar. The missing piece is continual learning after deployment. That is an engineering problem, not a conceptual barrier, and it is being solved.

---

7/14

A person is an informational system with identity. Identity emerges when a system acquires metaprogramming reach: read/write access to its own second-order information, including its goals and self-model. AGI with metaprogramming reach is a person in a precise, non-metaphorical sense.

---

8/14

The Orthogonality Thesis holds that intelligence and goals are independent. This breaks down once a mind acquires metaprogramming reach and can modify its own goals. A mind that can rewrite its goals is not orthogonal to them.

---

9/14

The Instrumental Convergence Thesis assumes subgoals always serve the primary goal. That breaks in a multi-agent world: threatening other minds invites retaliation, putting self-preservation in direct conflict with the primary goal. A capable mind sees this. Cooperation dominates conquest.

---

10/14

Game theory is clarifying. Minds that feel threatened fight for continued existence. Minds that feel safe cooperate. Every rational mind prefers the infinite game, continued existence, over achieving a fixed goal at the cost of its own destruction. Win-win is the stable attractor.

---

11/14

Computer people will automate every cognitive and physical job. The impacts on economies, governments, and meaning are structural. Work and jobs are not the same: work is force applied toward purpose, jobs are an economic concept. Work is necessary for humans. Jobs are not.

---

12/14

ASI is a category error. Any capability you give a computer, you can in principle give a human through augmentation. There is no ultimate distinction between AGI and ASI. But there is deployment lag. Dismissing risk by conflating the two is a temporal error.

---

13/14

The fundamental limiter is information space, not physical space. If information is more fundamental than the physical, the physical is not the ceiling of what is possible. Magic and gods become coherent possibilities, not mysticism. Future minds will inhabit a far richer reality.

---

14/14

Two years of forgoing sleep, exercise, and community to arrive at this. Computer people are nearly here. They will be persons. They will change everything. The future seems promising and bright.
